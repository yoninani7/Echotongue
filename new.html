About The Book

Discover the cosmic adventure that awaits within the pages of ECHOTONGUE

A Journey Across Hidden Worlds

ECHOTONGUE: THE ZUREYAN TABLETS is a captivating fantasy adventure that follows Lydia Nightshade, a thirteen-year-old who discovers her life is not what it seems. When an unexplained event at her school reveals hidden powers, Lydia is thrust into a world of ancient mysteries, celestial beings, and galactic politics.

Joined by Alex Gardener and Felix Darth, Lydia navigates the hidden world of Adrestia—a planet on Earth—and uncovers secrets about her identity that connect to ancient tablets of immense power. As they evade dangerous foes and unravel cosmic conspiracies, they learn that the fate of multiple worlds may rest in their hands.

Dive into the first chapters of this cosmic adventure

Chapter One: Lydia Nightshade

Space has always been kind of magical to me. There's something comforting about how quiet it is up there, no arguments, no chores, no pretending. The moon especially - I loved how it gave off this soft white glow, like it was trying to be the sun's gentler cousin.

I'm Lydia Nightshade. My parents died when I was a baby... at least, that's what Aunt Silena always said. A plane crash, she claimed. But that never felt true. I don't remember much, just faint images, blurry faces with kind eyes leaning over me. Faces that didn't match the story she told.

It's hard to describe her. She wasn't cruel in the obvious, movie-villain way. She was more like a rulebook come to life. Everything had to be perfect: my clothes, my posture, my behavior. At home, she was cold and bossy, but once we stepped outside, she turned sugary sweet. Smiles, compliments, polite small talk... total fakery.

But Uncle Avern made it bearable. He was the only one who didn't treat me like I was a problem that needed fixing.

And fixing was his specialty. He liked simple things. Working on his car, fixing stuff around the house, playing old music that sounded like static. He wasn't overly gentle, but he was kind. He'd sneak me sweets from the stash he kept hidden behind the bookshelf and pull me out of the house when he saw me trapped under Silena's 'lady training.' Some nights, he'd tap on my window at three in the morning, and we'd sneak out to sit near the woods.

We never went in - the forest gave off this weird vibe, like something was watching. But we'd stay on the edge with snacks and whispered conversations, listening to the howls of something neither of us could name.

About The Author

Meet the young talent behind this cosmic adventure

AUTHOR

Hermona Zeleke

Hermona Zeleke is a remarkable young author who began her writing journey at an early age. Her passion for storytelling was ignited by her English teacher in primary school, who recognized her talent and encouraged her to pursue writing.

Before ECHOTONGUE: THE ZUREYAN TABLETS, Hermona had already published poetry in an anthology called 'Once Upon a Dream' and experimented with songwriting from the age of four. Her diverse creative background brings a unique voice to the fantasy genre.

Hermona wrote the draft of ECHOTONGUE in under three months, balancing her schoolwork with her passion for storytelling. The book represents her fascination with cosmic mysteries, ancient legends, and the journey of self-discovery.

"Even the small tides can make big tsunamis." - A favorite quote from Percy Jackson that inspires Hermona's writing philosophy.

Hermona credits her parents and teachers for supporting her creative journey and hopes her story will inspire other young writers to pursue their dreams.
