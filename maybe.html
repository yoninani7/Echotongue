THE ZUREYAN TABLETS

What if everything you knew about yourself was a carefully constructed lie? When an ordinary school day erupts in inexplicable flames, thirteen-year-old Lydia Nightshade discovers she possesses impossible abilities. Thrust into a hidden world where language is power and planets hold ancient secrets, she must unravel the mystery of her origins while evading forces that would kill to control her awakening power.

The Awakening

There are thirty-two known worlds in the universe. Thirty-one of them are hidden from human eyes. One girl is about to discover she doesn't belong to any of them.

The Hidden Truth

Lydia Nightshade lived an ordinary life until the fire spoke through her. Now she's learning that "human" was just a disguise—and her real identity is a secret worth killing for.

The Forbidden Language

In a universe where words hold power, Echotongue is the most dangerous language of all. Spoken only in legends, it can reshape reality—or destroy it.

The Ancient Council

Eight powerful beings govern the thirty-two worlds from shadows. They maintain order through secrets, lies, and ancient laws that were meant to be forgotten.

The Thirty-Two Worlds

Each world holds its own secrets, its own magic, and its own truth. Some are whispered about in legends. Others are hidden in plain sight.

Those Who Walk Between Worlds

In a universe of secrets, trust is the rarest currency. These are the souls who will determine the fate of all thirty-two worlds.

The Zureyan Tablets

Three ancient stones. One contains the forgotten past. One records the hidden present. One reveals the impossible future. All three have been lost for millennia. Until now.

I

The Past Tablet

Contains truths about the creation of the thirty-two worlds that were meant to be erased from existence. What really happened at the beginning of everything?

II

The Present Tablet

Reveals the hidden connections between all living beings across worlds. Who is really pulling the strings in the Galactic Council?

III

The Future Tablet

Shows the destiny that awaits all thirty-two worlds. Will there be unity or destruction? The answer is written in a language no one fully understands.

The Ancient Riddle

Before the Tablets were lost, their keeper left behind a riddle. Solve it, and you'll understand why Echotongue must never be fully awakened.

"I am spoken but never heard, written but never read. I build worlds and tear them down. I am the first secret and the last truth. The wise fear me, the foolish seek me, and the powerful would destroy the universe to control me. What am I?"

The Oracle

Hermona Zeleke began hearing the whispers of other worlds at a young age. Her first published poem appeared in 'Once Upon a Dream' when she was just ten years old. At four, she wrote her first song—a melody that her entire school performed.

Echotongue: The Zureyan Tablets represents her journey into the deepest mysteries of language, power, and identity. Written in under three months while balancing schoolwork, this novel is proof that the greatest stories come from listening to the voices others cannot hear.
